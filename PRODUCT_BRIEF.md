# Budget Tracker — Product Brief

## What it is

A personal, offline-first budget tracking tool designed for daily use on mobile. It gives a continuous, honest picture of where your money goes — without requiring you to enter every purchase in detail or reconcile with a bank account.

## The problem it solves

Most budgeting tools ask you to categorise every transaction, set up accounts, and review dashboards. The overhead is high enough that people stop using them. This tool takes the opposite approach: logging a small purchase should take one tap, and the system should do the mental work of telling you whether you're on track.

## The three-segment model

Spending is split into three distinct buckets, because they behave differently and require different habits:

- **Daily** — Discretionary day-to-day spending (food, coffee, small purchases). This is the bucket you actively manage. It uses a 30-day rolling window, so the picture stays current without requiring month-end resets.

- **Bills** — Recurring fixed costs (rent, subscriptions, utilities). These are logged once and stay until they change. The total reflects your real monthly fixed-cost baseline.

- **Specials** — Large or irregular one-off expenses (travel, electronics, medical). Because these are lumpy, the tool spreads them across 12 months to show their true monthly cost impact.

The sum of all three segments gives you your actual total monthly cost of living.

## Core capabilities

**Frictionless daily logging** — Daily expenses can be entered with a single tap using preset amounts. The app accumulates rapid taps before committing, so logging three items in a row takes three taps, not three separate actions.

**Budget pacing** — For the daily segment, the app tracks whether spending is ahead or behind the expected pace at any given point in the month, and quantifies the gap in both euros and days of budget. This answers the practical question: "can I afford to spend normally today?"

**Spending breakdown** — Daily spending is automatically broken down by category (home food, eating out, shopping, entertainment, miscellaneous) to show where discretionary money actually goes over the past 30 days.

**Income tracking** — All three segments support income entries, which offset expenses. This handles reimbursements, refunds, and variable income without needing separate accounts.

**Cross-device sync** — Data can be exported as a URL and imported on another device, enabling sync between a phone and a tablet without requiring an account or a server.

**Offline operation** — The app works fully without a network connection and can be installed as a home-screen app on any mobile device.

## Who it's for

Someone who wants to stay broadly within a monthly spending target without the overhead of full double-entry bookkeeping or subscription finance software. The primary use is a quick daily check-in: log what you've spent, glance at whether you're on track, move on.
